Amelia Earhart was born in Atchison, Kansas on July 24, 1897 to a railroad attorney and his wife. A tomboy by nature, she independently spent her time playing sports, running, climbing trees, and imagining around her childhood towns of Atchison, and Kansas City, Kansas and Des Moines, Iowa.

She disappeared in 1937 during her transcontinental flight from Oakland, California to Honolulu Hawaii. Some people believe she ran out of fuel and was thus swallowed up by the sea, or had crashed into an island. Before her death, she had covered more than two-thirds of her last flight. To this day, her death is shrouded in mystery.
