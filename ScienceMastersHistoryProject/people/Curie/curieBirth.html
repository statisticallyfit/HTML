Marie Curie was born Maria Salomea Sklodowska in Warsaw, Poland on November 7, 1867. At that time, Poland was under the fierce rule of Russian authorities, a fact begrudged by Maria and her family. Maria had four siblings: Zosia, Hela, Jozef, and Bronya. Zosia died of typhus when Maria was merely eight, which was followed by her mother's death from tuberculosis. However, the rest of her family, fueled by their father's passion for science, went on to intellectual pursuits.

Marie Curie died in Savoy, France on July 4, 1934 from aplastic anemia, a bone marrow disease. It is generally believed that her constant exposure to radioactivity caused this disease. Her notes are so radioactive that they are stored in specially-made boxes and must be opened only by people using protective suits.
