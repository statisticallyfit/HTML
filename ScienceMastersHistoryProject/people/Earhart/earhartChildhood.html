As a young girl, Amelia Earhart lived mainly with her mother's parents in Atchison during school, and spent the summers with her parents in Kansas City. Amelia's parents, Alfred and Amelia Otis, lived a relatively rich life since her father was a district court judge, bank president, and a church warden.

Her independent nature drove her to be a roamer and adventurer - she spent her time "riding imaginary horses, climbing trees, sledding, and hunting" - despite her grandmother's disapproval. Ladies and even young girls of the time were expected to behave in a "genteel and ladylike fashion", but Amelia was the glaring exception. Her analytical mind led her to be amazed by mechanical things, and so, combining her mischievous nature and intelligence, she designed a trap to catch wandering chickens. Moreover, her non-conformity was even more evident as she willingly played sports and games meant only for boys.
