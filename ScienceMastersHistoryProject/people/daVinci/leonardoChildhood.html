Leonardo da Vinci

Childhood


Leonardo da Vinci was the illegitimate child of Ser Piero da Vinci, a well-respected notary who certified legal documents, and a commoner, Caterina. Leonardo was raised by his father, several stepmothers, uncle, and grandparents.

As a child, other than learning the rudiments of reading, writing, and mathematics, Leonardo did not receive a formal education. However, he proved to be a quick learner at arithmetic and music. Many noted his beautiful singing voice as he played his lyre. His childhood passion involved studying nature deeply and sketching its aspects in his diary.
